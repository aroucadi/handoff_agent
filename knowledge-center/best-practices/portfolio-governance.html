Portfolio Governance Framework

Establish decision-making frameworks, stage-gate reviews, and investment oversight using ClawdView Portfolios to maximize portfolio ROI.

Why Portfolio Governance Matters

Without governance, portfolios drift: low-value projects consume resources while strategic initiatives starve. ClawdView Portfolios provides the tooling, but governance provides the decision-making discipline. Organizations with mature governance frameworks deliver 30-40% more strategic value from their portfolios (source: PMI Pulse of the Profession 2025).

Governance Model

Three-Tier Decision Structure

Tier Forum Decisions Cadence
Strategic Investment Committee Portfolio priorities, budget allocation, strategic themes Quarterly
Tactical Portfolio Review Board Initiative approval, stage-gate reviews, resource rebalancing Monthly
Operational PMO Operations Status reporting, risk escalation, process compliance Weekly

Stage-Gate Process

Configure a 5-gate lifecycle in Portfolios → Governance → Stages:

  • Gate 0 — Idea Screening: Does the idea align with strategic themes? Criteria: strategic fit score > 3/5, sponsor identified.
  • Gate 1 — Business Case: Is the investment justified? Criteria: NPV > $100K, payback period < 18 months, risk assessment complete.
  • Gate 2 — Planning Approval: Is the plan feasible? Criteria: resource availability confirmed, dependencies mapped, risk mitigations plan accepted.
  • Gate 3 — Execution Checkpoint: Is the project on track? Criteria: CPI > 0.9, SPI > 0.85, no critical risks unmitigated.
  • Gate 4 — Closure & Benefits: Did we achieve the expected outcomes? Criteria: benefits realization report, lessons learned captured, resources released.

Scoring Model Design

Design your scoring model with these principles:

  • Use 5-7 criteria maximum — more causes decision fatigue
  • Weight criteria to reflect organizational priorities (e.g., strategic alignment 30%, ROI 25%, risk 20%, resource availability 15%, urgency 10%)
  • Use a consistent 1-5 scale with anchored descriptions for each level
  • Include at least one must-pass criterion (e.g., regulatory compliance) that can veto regardless of total score
💡 Pro Tip

Review and recalibrate scoring weights annually with your Investment Committee. A shift in strategy (e.g., from growth to efficiency) should be reflected in the scoring model. ClawdView Portfolios maintains scoring history, so you can compare how the same initiative would rank under old vs. new weights.

Common Governance Failures

  • Zombie Projects: Projects that should be killed but nobody has the authority or incentive to cancel them. Solution: mandatory quarterly portfolio review with explicit "continue/pivot/kill" decisions.
  • Underfunded Winners: High-value initiatives that receive minimum viable resources. Solution: dynamic resource reallocation triggers when portfolio scores change.
  • Gate Gaming: Teams that inflate metrics to pass stage gates. Solution: independent verification using data from PPM Pro and AgilePlace delivery metrics.
