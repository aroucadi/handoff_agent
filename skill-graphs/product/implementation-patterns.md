# VeloSaaS Implementation Patterns

## Phased Approach (Recommended)

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)**: Product catalog migration, user provisioning, basic pricing rules. Target: sales team can create simple quotes.

**Phase 2 — Optimization (Weeks 5–8)**: Complex pricing logic, approval workflows, ERP integration testing. Target: end-to-end quote-to-cash flow working.

**Phase 3 — Adoption (Weeks 9–12)**: Training, change management, parallel running with legacy system. Target: 80% sales team adoption.

## By Deal Size

- **< $500K deals**: Lightweight implementation, 60-day target. Self-service onboarding with CSM check-ins.
- **$500K–$2M deals**: Standard implementation, 90-day target. Dedicated CSM + implementation consultant.
- **> $2M deals**: Enterprise implementation, 120–180 day target. Full project team with technical architect.

## By Vertical

- **Manufacturing**: Longest implementations due to complex product catalogs and ERP dependencies. Typical: 90–120 days. Key risk: SAP/Oracle integration timelines.
- **Financial Services**: Moderate complexity. Heavy compliance requirements but simpler product catalogs. Typical: 75–90 days.
- **Technology/SaaS**: Fastest implementations. Subscription-native pricing. Typical: 45–60 days.

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Executive sponsor engaged** — weekly 15-min check-in with VP+ level
2. **Product catalog clean** — data quality is the #1 determinant of timeline accuracy
3. **Change management started early** — sales team resistance is the #1 cause of failed [[typical-timelines]]
